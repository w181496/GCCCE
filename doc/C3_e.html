GCCCE 2015 Call for Papers

 

Conference: The Global Chinese Conference on Computers in Education(GCCCE) 2015

Sub-conference 3: Learning Analytics and Assessment

Conference date: 25 (Monday) - 29 (Friday) May 2015

Conference venue: National Central University, Tai Pei

 

With the development of the Internet and technology, joyful learning has gradually become an important research hotpot in the field of educational technology, and has captured lots of attention of the academe, the enterprise, and even the politicians. The development of information technology and the society has brought about the demand for innovative talents. As the learning style is continuously changing, the idea of providing a pleasant studying environment and cultivating innovative intellectuals becomes a common sense and social pursuit. Joyful learning will have a broad perspective in the future.  

However, although the playing of games makes great difference in education, educational games still face with many difficulties. There will be a while when the parents and teachers finally accept this learning style. What’s more, there is not an authoritative standard to evaluate joyful learning and no enough theories to instruct the practice, and we still have a long way to go.

This sub-conference expects to invite people from all fields who are interested in our topic and share their experience. In particular, this sub-conference also welcomes the business communities.

 
The scope of papers will cover but not be limited to:

·Joyful learning theories

·Design of joyful learning activities

·Advanced joyful learning technologies (e.g., artificial intelligence, personalization, adaptive system, cross-platform gaming mechanism, augmented reality, etc.)

·Design and development of joyful learning

·Applications of joyful learning systems

·Comparison of joyful learning systems

·Effectiveness of joyful learning

·Joyful learning and education innovation

·Joyful learning and high-order skills (problem-solving skills, creative ability, et.)

·Joyful learning, social and culture

·Joyful learning and adolescent development

·Successful cases of joyful learning

·Difficulty and obstacles that joyful learning research encounter

 

Paper submission:

Authors should prepare submissions either in English or in Chinese (Long paper: 8 pages; Short paper: 4 pages; Poster: 2 pages). Submissions written in Chinese should include the title, abstract and keywords written in both Chinese and English.

Authors should submit papers in the Microsoft Word format. Please make use of the paper template provided on the conference website for preparing submissions.

Authors should make submissions by uploading papers onto the Submission System of the conference.

 

Potential journal publication

Submissions to this sub-conference will be recommended to publish in Global Chinese Journal for Computers in Education (in Chinese) (http://www.gcjce.net),  International Journal on Digital Learning Technology (in Chinese) (http://www.ijdlt.org), Journal of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences (in Chinese) (http://jlass.ntust.edu.tw/index.php/lass) and Journal of Computers in Education (in English).
